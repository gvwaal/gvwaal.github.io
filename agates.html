Optimal Shore-based Agate Picking Areas of NE MN

The Lake Superior Agate is a semiprecious gemstone and the state gemstone of Minnesota, characterized by red, orange, or yellow bands of jasper and chalcedony with variable coarse quartz crystals. While found throughout the Lake Superior basin, the Minnesota's North Shore is a frequent destination for those in search of agates. Having lived in Duluth, MN for around five years, a frequent question from visiting friends and family is "Where are the best places to look for agates?" The answer to their question is gravel pits, but that's not the answer they're looking for. This project aims to identify the best locations for agate hunting on the North Shore from a quantitative geologic viewpoint, taking into account hard data

Data acquisition

This project relied primarily on data created and hosted by the State of Minnesota, with lesser amounts from the Minnesota Geological Survey and private persons.

quadrangles to 1m rasters: e00 to coverage coverage to feature class polygon to raster, using maplabel as the field, maximum_area, cellsize = 1

Data used:

Scientific and Natural Areas - https://gisdata.mn.gov/dataset/bdry-scientific-and-nat-areas
State Parks - https://gisdata.mn.gov/dataset/bdry-dnr-lrs-prk
Roads - https://gisdata.mn.gov/dataset/trans-roads-mndot-tis
Lake Superior - https://gisdata.mn.gov/dataset/water-lake-superior-basin
Digital Elevation Models - ftp://ftp.lmic.state.mn.us/pub/data/elevation/lidar/
Bedrock quadrangles - https://conservancy.umn.edu/handle/11299/708/discover?query=m-&filter=bedrock - of 16 quadrangles downloaded, 3 had modern GIS layers (shapefiles), 11 had legacy GIS layers (e00) (7 requiring conversion from interchange to coverage), and the rest have scanned PDFs (650 DPI based on https://gis.stackexchange.com/a/41464, 1000 DPI caused issues with sections not being converted, rgb palette, no compression) silver bay control points "colinear"
Grand Portage reservation boundary - http://www.deadpioneer.com/routes/US61/historicus61se/mineralcenter/mineralcentercontext.jpg - georeferenced image based on the points in gp_georef_points.txt and traced the res/mn border when creating a polygon. attempted to follow the hash marks in the middle of the reservation boundary
Minnesota Composite Image service - http://www.mngeo.state.mn.us/chouse/wms/composite_image.html
